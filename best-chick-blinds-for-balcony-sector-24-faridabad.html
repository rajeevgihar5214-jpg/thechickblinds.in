Balcony Blind Selection

How to Choose the Best Chick Blind for a Balcony

Check Balcony Coverage

Natural bamboo is more suitable when the balcony has adequate roof coverage and limited direct exposure to rainfall.

Check Sunlight Direction

West-facing balconies may require a closer bamboo weave or optional suitable backing for stronger afternoon sunlight.

Check Wind Exposure

Open balcony sides may require narrower blind panels, stronger fittings and suitable lower support to reduce movement.

Select Privacy Level

A closer weave can provide better daytime privacy than a loose or widely spaced bamboo weave.

Divide Wide Openings

Natural bamboo blinds are suitable for living rooms, bedrooms, dining spaces, indoor windows and protected door openings.

Covered balcony use

Natural bamboo blinds can be used in covered or semi-protected balconies after checking wind and occasional rain splash.

Open balcony exposure

Untreated natural bamboo may be affected by continuous direct rain, moisture and harsh weather exposure.

Untreated natural bamboo blinds should not be described as fully waterproof. PVC or another suitable weather-resistant chick blind is better for regular rain exposure.
