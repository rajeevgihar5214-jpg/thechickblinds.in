Patio Selection Guidance

Choosing the Correct Bamboo Blind for Your Patio

Check the patio roof coverage

A properly covered patio is generally more suitable for natural bamboo than an opening receiving continuous direct rain.

Check sunlight direction

West-facing patios may require a closer bamboo weave or suitable privacy backing for stronger afternoon sunlight.

Check wind exposure

Open-side patios may receive stronger wind and can require smaller blind panels, stronger brackets and bottom support.

Check mounting support

The wall, beam, ceiling or frame should be suitable for the selected blind width and operating arrangement.

Select the required privacy level

The bamboo weave, border and optional backing should be selected according to the desired sunlight and privacy control.

Keep everyday access practical

Doorways and frequently used patio sections should have separate blinds so daily movement remains convenient.

Material and Weather Guidance

Natural Bamboo Blinds and Waterproof Alternatives

Covered patio use

Natural bamboo blinds work well in covered patio areas after checking wind exposure and the direction of occasional rain splash.

Semi-protected patio use

Semi-protected patios should be inspected carefully because strong wind and side rain may affect natural bamboo material.

Open patio use

Untreated bamboo can be affected by continuous rain, moisture and harsh outdoor exposure. Open patios may require a weather-resistant alternative.

PVC waterproof alternative

Natural bamboo blinds should not be described as fully waterproof. PVC or suitable weather-resistant chick blinds are more practical for openings regularly exposed to moisture.
