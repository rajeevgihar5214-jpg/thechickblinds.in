Product Difference

Zebra Blinds, Wooden Blinds and Bamboo Blinds Explained

Zebra Blinds

Zebra blinds are made with alternating sheer and opaque fabric bands. The bands can be aligned to adjust daylight and daytime privacy.

Wooden-Look Zebra Blinds

Wooden-look zebra blinds use fabric in brown, beige or wood-inspired colours. They are not made from solid wooden slats.

Real Wooden Blinds

Real or engineered wooden blinds generally use horizontal slats that tilt for light control. They are a different product from zebra blinds.

Natural Bamboo Blinds

Natural bamboo blinds are woven or tied bamboo products with a traditional roll-up appearance and natural texture.

Best for Modern Interiors

Zebra blinds are generally selected for contemporary living rooms, bedrooms, offices and modern apartment interiors.

Best for Natural Styling

Natural bamboo blinds are generally selected for earthy décor, covered balconies, porches, windows and traditional interiors.

Material and Usage Guidance

Correct Usage of Zebra and Natural Bamboo Blinds

Indoor zebra blind use

Zebra blinds are generally designed for indoor windows, living rooms, bedrooms, offices and protected balcony-facing openings.

Zebra blinds and moisture

Fabric zebra blinds should remain protected from direct rain, continuous moisture, heavy dust and harsh outdoor exposure.

Indoor natural bamboo use

Natural bamboo blinds are suitable for bedrooms, living rooms, dining spaces, indoor windows and other protected openings.

Covered balcony bamboo blinds

Natural bamboo can be used in covered or semi-protected balconies after checking wind and occasional rain splash.

Are natural bamboo blinds waterproof?

Untreated natural bamboo blinds are not fully waterproof. PVC or another suitable weather-resistant blind is better for regular rain exposure.

Are zebra blinds blackout blinds?

Standard zebra blinds provide adjustable light control but should not automatically be treated as complete blackout blinds. Blackout performance depends on the selected product.
